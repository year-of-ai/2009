## Notable Events of 2009

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| [Barack Obama inaugurated](history-politics/obama-inauguration-2009.md) | Democrat Barack Obama was sworn in as the 44th President of the United States on January 20, becoming the first African American to hold the office. |
| [Miracle on the Hudson](history-politics/miracle-on-the-hudson.md) | US Airways Flight 1549 ditched in the Hudson River on January 15 after a bird strike disabled both engines; all 155 aboard survived, making it one of aviation history's most celebrated emergency landings. |
| [H1N1 swine flu pandemic](science-technology/h1n1-swine-flu-pandemic.md) | A novel H1N1 influenza strain emerged in Mexico and the United States in April; the WHO declared a Phase 6 pandemic on June 11 — the first influenza pandemic since 1968 — affecting nearly every country. |
| [Iran's Green Movement protests](history-politics/iran-green-movement-2009.md) | Disputed presidential election results in June triggered mass protests under the banner of the Green Movement, the largest civil unrest in Iran since the 1979 revolution, met with violent suppression. |
| [Michael Jackson dies](arts-culture/michael-jackson-death-2009.md) | Pop star Michael Jackson died of acute propofol intoxication on June 25 at age 50, prompting a global outpouring of grief and renewed recognition of his cultural legacy. |
| [Uyghur unrest in Xinjiang](society-economics/uyghur-unrest-xinjiang-2009.md) | Deadly ethnic riots broke out in Ürümqi on July 5, leaving at least 197 dead and over 1,700 injured, drawing international attention to tensions between the Han and Uyghur communities in western China. |
| [Barack Obama awarded Nobel Peace Prize](history-politics/obama-nobel-peace-prize-2009.md) | The Norwegian Nobel Committee awarded Barack Obama the 2009 Peace Prize on October 9, citing his efforts to strengthen international diplomacy and nuclear disarmament, in a decision that sparked broad debate. |
| [Sonia Sotomayor confirmed to Supreme Court](history-politics/sotomayor-supreme-court-2009.md) | The Senate confirmed Sonia Sotomayor 68–31 on August 6, making her the first Hispanic and third woman to serve as a US Supreme Court justice. |
| [Fort Hood shooting](history-politics/fort-hood-shooting-2009.md) | US Army psychiatrist Nidal Hasan opened fire at Fort Hood, Texas on November 5, killing 13 people and wounding 30 in the deadliest attack on a US military base in history. |
| [Copenhagen Climate Conference (COP15)](history-politics/cop15-copenhagen-2009.md) | The 15th UN Climate Change Conference convened in Copenhagen from December 7–18, drawing 115 world leaders in an attempt to forge a binding successor to the Kyoto Protocol; the resulting Copenhagen Accord fell short of a legally binding treaty. |
| [*Avatar* theatrical release](arts-culture/avatar-2009.md) | James Cameron's *Avatar* opened on December 18, becoming the highest-grossing film of all time (surpassing *Titanic*) and a landmark in the adoption of 3D cinema technology. |
| [American Recovery and Reinvestment Act](society-economics/american-recovery-act-2009.md) | President Obama signed the $787 billion economic stimulus package on February 17, the largest fiscal intervention in US history to that point, aimed at reversing the deepest recession since the Great Depression. |
| [LHC first proton collisions](science-technology/lhc-first-proton-collisions-2009.md) | The Large Hadron Collider restarted on November 20 after its 2008 quench incident and achieved first proton-proton collisions at 900 GeV on November 23, resuming the world's premier particle physics programme. |
| [Bitcoin genesis block mined](science-technology/bitcoin-genesis-block-2009.md) | Satoshi Nakamoto mined the first Bitcoin block (Block 0) on January 3, launching the world's first decentralized cryptocurrency and inaugurating a new era in digital finance. |
