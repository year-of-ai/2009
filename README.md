## Notable Events of 2009

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| [Barack Obama inaugurated]({{ '/news/history-politics/obama-inauguration-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Democrat Barack Obama was sworn in as the 44th President of the United States on January 20, becoming the first African American to hold the office. |
| [Miracle on the Hudson]({{ '/news/history-politics/miracle-on-the-hudson/' | relative_url }}) | US Airways Flight 1549 ditched in the Hudson River on January 15 after a bird strike disabled both engines; all 155 aboard survived, making it one of aviation history's most celebrated emergency landings. |
| [Gaza War and Ceasefire (2008–2009)]({{ '/news/history-politics/gaza-war-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Israel's 22-day "Operation Cast Lead" military campaign against Hamas and militant groups in the Gaza Strip concluded with a ceasefire on January 18, killing over 1,100 Palestinians (roughly half civilians) and causing mass displacement in the densely populated enclave. |
| [H1N1 swine flu pandemic]({{ '/news/science-technology/h1n1-swine-flu-pandemic/' | relative_url }}) | A novel H1N1 influenza strain emerged in Mexico and the United States in April; the WHO declared a Phase 6 pandemic on June 11 — the first influenza pandemic since 1968 — affecting nearly every country. |
| [2009 L'Aquila earthquake]({{ '/news/society-economics/laquila-earthquake-2009/' | relative_url }}) | A magnitude-6.3 earthquake killed 308 in L'Aquila, Italy on April 6, devastating the medieval city and prompting Italy to relocate July's G8 summit there. |
| [Iran's Green Movement protests]({{ '/news/history-politics/iran-green-movement-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Disputed presidential election results in June triggered mass protests under the banner of the Green Movement, the largest civil unrest in Iran since the 1979 revolution, met with violent suppression. |
| [2009 Honduran coup d'état]({{ '/news/history-politics/honduran-coup-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Soldiers removed President Manuel Zelaya on June 28, installing Roberto Micheletti and triggering the OAS's first-ever member-state suspension under its Democratic Charter. |
| [Michael Jackson dies]({{ '/news/arts-culture/michael-jackson-death-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Pop star Michael Jackson died of acute propofol intoxication on June 25 at age 50, prompting a global outpouring of grief and renewed recognition of his cultural legacy. |
| [Uyghur unrest in Xinjiang]({{ '/news/society-economics/uyghur-unrest-xinjiang-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Deadly ethnic riots broke out in Ürümqi on July 5, leaving at least 197 dead and over 1,700 injured, drawing international attention to tensions between the Han and Uyghur communities in western China. |
| [Roger Federer's 15th Grand Slam at Wimbledon]({{ '/news/people/roger-federer-15th-grand-slam-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Swiss champion Roger Federer won his sixth Wimbledon title on July 5, defeating Andy Roddick 5–7, 7–6(6), 7–6(5), 3–6, 16–14; the victory marked his 15th major, breaking Pete Sampras's Open Era record. |
| [Michael Jackson Memorial Service]({{ '/news/arts-culture/michael-jackson-memorial-service-2009/' | relative_url }}) | A public memorial service for Michael Jackson was held July 7 at Los Angeles's Staples Center, drawing an estimated 1.6 million ticket requests and reaching 2.5+ billion global television viewers, reflecting his unprecedented cultural influence. |
| [Mumbai terror attacks aftermath (2009)]({{ '/news/history-politics/mumbai-26-11-aftermath-2009/' | relative_url }}) | The sole surviving Mumbai attack gunman, Ajmal Amir Kasab, faced trial in July 2009, confessing to Pakistan-based Lashkar-e-Taiba training and revealing state-sponsored operational links; his conviction and 2009 trial exposed Pakistan's intelligence role in the November 2008 attacks that killed 166. |
| [Barack Obama awarded Nobel Peace Prize]({{ '/news/history-politics/obama-nobel-peace-prize-2009/' | relative_url }}) | The Norwegian Nobel Committee awarded Barack Obama the 2009 Peace Prize on October 9, citing his efforts to strengthen international diplomacy and nuclear disarmament, in a decision that sparked broad debate. |
| [Sonia Sotomayor confirmed to Supreme Court]({{ '/news/history-politics/sotomayor-supreme-court-2009/' | relative_url }}) | The Senate confirmed Sonia Sotomayor 68–31 on August 6, making her the first Hispanic and third woman to serve as a US Supreme Court justice. |
| [Healthcare reform and Ted Kennedy's legacy]({{ '/news/history-politics/healthcare-reform-ted-kennedy-legacy-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Following Senator Ted Kennedy's death on August 25 from brain cancer, healthcare reform moved to the center of Obama's legislative agenda; his legacy became pivotal to shaping the emerging Affordable Care Act framework. |
| [Fort Hood shooting]({{ '/news/history-politics/fort-hood-shooting-2009/' | relative_url }}) | US Army psychiatrist Nidal Hasan opened fire at Fort Hood, Texas on November 5, killing 13 people and wounding 30 in the deadliest attack on a US military base in history. |
| [Amazon Kindle e-reader expansion]({{ '/news/science-technology/kindle-ereader-launch-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Amazon released the redesigned Kindle 2 in February and the large-screen Kindle DX in June, establishing the e-reader as a mass-market device and accelerating publishing's shift from print to digital. |
| [Copenhagen Climate Conference (COP15)]({{ '/news/history-politics/cop15-copenhagen-2009/' | relative_url }}) | The 15th UN Climate Change Conference convened in Copenhagen from December 7–18, drawing 115 world leaders in an attempt to forge a binding successor to the Kyoto Protocol; the resulting Copenhagen Accord fell short of a legally binding treaty. |
| [*Avatar* theatrical release]({{ '/news/arts-culture/avatar-2009/' | relative_url }}) | James Cameron's *Avatar* opened on December 18, becoming the highest-grossing film of all time (surpassing *Titanic*) and a landmark in the adoption of 3D cinema technology. |
| [American Recovery and Reinvestment Act]({{ '/news/society-economics/american-recovery-act-2009/' | relative_url }}) | President Obama signed the $787 billion economic stimulus package on February 17, the largest fiscal intervention in US history to that point, aimed at reversing the deepest recession since the Great Depression. |
| [Cyclone Nargis humanitarian aftermath (2009)]({{ '/news/society-economics/cyclone-nargis-humanitarian-aftermath-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Myanmar's May 2008 Cyclone Nargis continued to devastate the country throughout 2009, with disease outbreaks, malnutrition, and slow reconstruction amid the junta's corruption and international aid obstruction, killing an estimated 138,000+ and displacing millions. |
| [LHC first proton collisions]({{ '/news/science-technology/lhc-first-proton-collisions-2009/' | relative_url }}) | The Large Hadron Collider restarted on November 20 after its 2008 quench incident and achieved first proton-proton collisions at 900 GeV on November 23, resuming the world's premier particle physics programme. |
| [Bitcoin genesis block mined]({{ '/news/science-technology/bitcoin-genesis-block-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Satoshi Nakamoto mined the first Bitcoin block (Block 0) on January 3, launching the world's first decentralized cryptocurrency and inaugurating a new era in digital finance. |
| [Kepler Space Telescope launched]({{ '/news/science-technology/kepler-space-telescope-launch-2009/' | relative_url }}) | NASA's Kepler Space Telescope launched on March 6 to search for Earth-sized exoplanets in habitable zones, ultimately discovering over 2,600 confirmed exoplanets and transforming humanity's understanding of planetary systems. |
| [*Watchmen* film adaptation]({{ '/news/arts-culture/watchmen-2009-film/' | relative_url }}) | Zack Snyder's live-action adaptation of the 1986 graphic novel released March 6, bringing the morally complex superhero story to cinema with visual fidelity and thematic depth, grossing $185.3 million worldwide. |
| [Up theatrical release]({{ '/news/arts-culture/up-pixar-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Pixar released *Up* on May 29, an animated feature film about an elderly widower and young explorer traveling to Paradise Falls; the film earned critical acclaim, a Best Picture Oscar nomination, and became a modern animation classic. |
| [SpaceX Falcon 9 development]({{ '/news/science-technology/spacex-falcon-9-first-flight-2009/' | relative_url }}) | SpaceX advanced its Falcon 9 rocket through 2009 — nine-engine static fires and a $1.6 billion NASA cargo contract — setting the stage for commercial spaceflight's transformation. |
| [General Motors Chapter 11 bankruptcy]({{ '/news/society-economics/general-motors-bankruptcy-2009/' | relative_url }}) | GM filed for bankruptcy on June 1 in a government-backed restructuring, one of the largest US corporate bankruptcies and a defining episode of the Great Recession. |
| [*Slumdog Millionaire* wins eight Oscars]({{ '/news/arts-culture/slumdog-millionaire-oscars-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Danny Boyle's film won eight Academy Awards including Best Picture on February 22, a landmark crossover success for internationally produced cinema. |
| [Ardi (*Ardipithecus ramidus*) unveiled]({{ '/news/science-technology/ardipithecus-ramidus-2009/' | relative_url }}) | *Science* published the first full analysis of the 4.4-million-year-old skeleton "Ardi" on October 1, reshaping understanding of early human evolution. |
| [Sri Lankan Civil War ends]({{ '/news/history-politics/sri-lankan-civil-war-end-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Government forces defeated the Tamil Tiger insurgency on May 18 and killed LTTE leader Velupillai Prabhakaran, ending a 26-year civil war in a decisive military victory. |
| [Bernie Madoff sentenced]({{ '/news/society-economics/madoff-sentencing-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Financier Bernie Madoff was sentenced to 150 years in prison on June 29 for the largest Ponzi scheme in history, the defining fraud of the Great Recession. |
| [Usain Bolt's 2009 world records]({{ '/news/people/usain-bolt-2009-world-records/' | relative_url }}) | Jamaican sprinter Usain Bolt set still-standing 100 m (9.58) and 200 m (19.19) world records at the Berlin World Championships in August, redefining the limits of human speed. |
| [51st Grammy Awards]({{ '/news/arts-culture/grammy-awards-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Robert Plant and Alison Krauss's *Raising Sand* won Album of the Year at the February 8 ceremony, which also named Adele Best New Artist. |
| [Google Chrome's 2009 rise]({{ '/news/science-technology/google-chrome-launch-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Google Chrome, launched as a beta in September 2008, matured rapidly through 2009 — shipping stable versions 2.0 and 3.0 and multiplying its market share on the strength of its V8 JavaScript engine and multi-process design. |
| [Kanye West interrupts Taylor Swift at the VMAs]({{ '/news/people/kanye-west-taylor-swift-vma-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Kanye West stormed the September 13 VMA stage during Taylor Swift's acceptance speech, sparking a defining celebrity feud of the social-media era. |
| [*Star Trek* film reboot]({{ '/news/arts-culture/star-trek-2009-film/' | relative_url }}) | J.J. Abrams' reboot opened May 8, reviving the dormant franchise via an alternate "Kelvin Timeline" and grossing $385.7 million worldwide. |
| [India's 2009 General Election]({{ '/news/history-politics/india-election-2009/' | relative_url }}) | India held the world's largest democratic election across April 16–May 16, and the Congress party secured an unexpected victory under PM Manmohan Singh, affirming democratic stability during global financial crisis. |
| [Susan Boyle's *Britain's Got Talent* breakthrough]({{ '/news/arts-culture/susan-boyle-bgt-2009/' | relative_url }}) | The Scottish singer's April 11 audition became an early viral-video phenomenon, drawing roughly 100 million views within a week. |
| [Sarah Palin resigns as Alaska governor]({{ '/news/history-politics/palin-alaska-resignation-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Governor Sarah Palin announced her mid-term resignation on July 3, effective July 26, repositioning herself as a national Tea Party figure. |
| [Serena Williams' Wimbledon Grand Slam victory]({{ '/news/people/serena-williams-wimbledon-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Serena Williams won her 11th Grand Slam singles title on July 4, defeating her sister Venus in the Wimbledon final, cementing her dominance in women's tennis and her status as a transformative athletic figure. |
| [Air France Flight 447 disaster]({{ '/news/history-politics/air-france-447-2009/' | relative_url }}) | An Airbus A330 crashed into the South Atlantic on June 1, killing all 228 aboard; the investigation reshaped aviation safety around pitot-tube icing and high-altitude stall recovery. |
| [Windows 7 general availability]({{ '/news/science-technology/windows-7-release-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Microsoft released Windows 7 on October 22, a well-received recovery from Windows Vista that became its fastest-selling operating system. |
| [Cash for Clunkers]({{ '/news/society-economics/cash-for-clunkers-2009/' | relative_url }}) | The $3 billion Car Allowance Rebate System ran July–August, scrapping roughly 677,000 vehicles to spur recession-era auto sales and improve fuel efficiency. |
| [Chrysler Chapter 11 bankruptcy]({{ '/news/society-economics/chrysler-bankruptcy-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Chrysler filed for bankruptcy on April 30 in a government-backed Section 363 sale that allied it with Fiat, setting the template for the GM reorganization weeks later. |
| [Death of Senator Ted Kennedy]({{ '/news/people/ted-kennedy-death-2009/' | relative_url }}) | The "Lion of the Senate" died of brain cancer on August 25 after nearly 47 years in office; the seat's loss in a January 2010 special election reshaped the health-care reform fight. |
| [Maersk Alabama hijacking]({{ '/news/history-politics/maersk-alabama-hijacking-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Somali pirates seized the US-flagged ship on April 8 and took Captain Richard Phillips hostage; Navy SEAL snipers freed him on April 12 in a standoff that spotlighted Somali piracy. |
| [Wolfram Alpha launches]({{ '/news/science-technology/wolfram-alpha-launch-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Wolfram Research's computational knowledge engine went live on May 18, pioneering a curated, computation-first alternative to search that answered natural-language questions with computed results. |
| [G20 Pittsburgh Summit]({{ '/news/history-politics/g20-pittsburgh-summit-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Leaders met September 24–25 and designated the G20 the premier forum for international economic cooperation, embedding emerging economies at the core of global financial governance. |
| [Death of Patrick Swayze]({{ '/news/people/patrick-swayze-death-2009/' | relative_url }}) | The *Dirty Dancing* and *Ghost* star died of pancreatic cancer on September 14 at age 57, drawing wide public grief and renewed attention to the disease. |
| [Microsoft Bing launches]({{ '/news/science-technology/microsoft-bing-launch-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Microsoft officially launched Bing on June 3 as a "decision engine" to replace Live Search, featuring visual search results and semantic query understanding in direct competition with Google's market dominance. |
| [*The Twilight Saga: New Moon*]({{ '/news/arts-culture/twilight-new-moon-2009/' | relative_url }}) | The second installment of the Twilight franchise opened November 20, breaking numerous box-office records and grossing about $710 million worldwide to rank among the year's top films, cementing teen vampire-romance as a defining 2000s cultural phenomenon. |
| [The Beatles 09/09/09 remasters]({{ '/news/arts-culture/beatles-remasters-2009/' | relative_url }}) | Apple Corps and EMI reissued the Beatles' entire catalogue in remastered stereo and mono editions on September 9, alongside *The Beatles: Rock Band*, in the most comprehensive overhaul of the recordings since 1987. |
