# End of the Sri Lankan Civil War

**Category**: History & Politics  
**Key figures**: Mahinda Rajapaksa (President of Sri Lanka), Velupillai Prabhakaran (leader of the LTTE), Sarath Fonseka (Commander of the Sri Lanka Army)

## Summary

The Sri Lankan Civil War ended on 18 May 2009, when government forces captured the last sliver of territory held by the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE) and killed the group's founder and leader, Velupillai Prabhakaran. President Mahinda Rajapaksa formally declared victory on 19 May, bringing to a close a conflict that had lasted some 26 years since 1983 and had cost an estimated 80,000 to 100,000 lives. The final government offensive, often designated Eelam War IV, ran from 2006 and concluded with the military destruction of the LTTE as a conventional fighting force.

## Background

The war pitted the Sinhalese-majority Sri Lankan state against the LTTE, a separatist insurgency that sought an independent Tamil homeland (Tamil Eelam) in the north and east of the island. From its emergence in the 1970s and the outbreak of full-scale war in 1983, the LTTE built a disciplined guerrilla and conventional force notorious for pioneering the modern use of suicide bombing and for assassinating political leaders, including Indian Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi (1991) and Sri Lankan President Ranasinghe Premadasa (1993). A 2002 Norwegian-brokered ceasefire collapsed, and the Rajapaksa government launched a sustained military campaign from 2006.

## The Final Offensive

Through 2008 and early 2009, the Sri Lanka Army steadily overran LTTE strongholds in the Northern Province, capturing the de facto rebel capital of Kilinochchi in January 2009. The remaining Tigers and a large civilian population were compressed into a shrinking coastal pocket in the Mullaitivu District. The closing weeks of the war were marked by intense controversy over civilian casualties: United Nations estimates later suggested that tens of thousands of civilians may have died in the final phase, and both the government and the LTTE were accused of serious violations of international humanitarian law. Prabhakaran was killed on 18 May 2009 while attempting to flee the battle zone, and his death was confirmed through DNA identification the following day.

## Significance

The defeat of the LTTE ended one of Asia's longest-running civil wars and dismantled an insurgency widely regarded as one of the world's most capable non-state military organizations. The victory consolidated the political dominance of President Rajapaksa but left enduring questions over wartime accountability, the humanitarian toll of the final offensive, and the unresolved political grievances of Sri Lanka's Tamil minority. Calls for international investigation into alleged war crimes persisted for years afterward, and post-war reconciliation remained a central challenge for the country.
