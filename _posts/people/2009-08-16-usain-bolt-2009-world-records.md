**Key figures**: Usain Bolt (Jamaican sprinter), Tyson Gay (American sprinter, 100 m runner-up)

## Summary

At the 2009 World Championships in Athletics in Berlin, Germany, Jamaican sprinter Usain Bolt set world records in both the 100 metres and the 200 metres. On 16 August 2009 he won the 100 m in 9.58 seconds, and on 20 August 2009 he won the 200 m in 19.19 seconds. Both marks remain the world records, cementing Bolt's status as the fastest sprinter in recorded history and the dominant track-and-field athlete of his era.

## The Berlin Performances

In the 100 m final, Bolt lowered his own world record of 9.69 — set at the 2008 Beijing Olympics — by 0.11 seconds, the largest single improvement in the 100 m world record since the introduction of fully automatic electronic timing. American Tyson Gay finished second in 9.71, itself a US record, underscoring the exceptional quality of the race. Four days later, Bolt won the 200 m in 19.19 seconds, taking 0.11 seconds off the 19.30 he had run in Beijing, despite a slight head wind.

Bolt, born on 21 August 1986 in Trelawny Parish, Jamaica, had emerged the previous year at the Beijing Olympics with three gold medals and three world records. The Berlin championships confirmed that his Olympic breakthrough was no anomaly and added the world titles to his Olympic crowns.

## Significance

Bolt's 9.58 and 19.19 redefined the perceived limits of human sprinting and stood unmatched for more than a decade and a half. The performances made him a global sporting icon and the centerpiece of athletics' commercial appeal during a period when the sport was contending with widespread doping scandals. His combination of dominance, longevity, and showmanship helped sustain mainstream interest in track and field, and the Berlin records became enduring benchmarks against which subsequent sprinters are measured.
